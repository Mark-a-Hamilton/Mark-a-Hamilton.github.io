Most of my IT career has been spent in support, as a result this has given me an insight into the perception from both the Customer & supplier where each has multiple levels (see the Customer & Supplier Tabs). The main difference is that the Customer is on the whole IT Illiterate unlike the Supplier. As a result this means that the Customer just wants a working software system while the development team is more concerned with getting the software up and running.

There are 3 Basic types of Customer though this will vary depending on the size of the operation

  1. Business Owners Are mainly concerned with returning the most profits with as little outlay as possible. This, also involves IT systems and unfortunately the business owners also hold the purse strings.
  2. Managers / Supervisors Are mainly concerned with hitting targets, as they have the business owners chasing for results. It is fair to say that some (though not all) are bullies, which means in some circumstances they believe that the louder you shout the more things will get done. In short this breed of manager / supervisor has no people skills, meaning as a support engineer I soon developed a thick skin and learned how to deal with such attitudes.
  3. Staff / Sales Assistants Ironically these are the users of a software system, have no control over the system and suffer the frustration if theres a problem due to a lack of understanding the problem. Also having their manager and/or supervisor trying to get them to make the system work adds to the pressure.

  1. Sales Staff are usually on commission / performance based pay to encourage sales. This results in empty promises, so in short customers make a request and sales staff will agree with the customer just to make a sale. As a support engineer I've had complaints from customers when they were promised functionality would be done but it hasn't.
  2. Developers are solely focussed on the software product.
  3. Support I accept my view is biased but it seems support are responsible for picking up the pieces.

The Deployment Strategy initially is to keep the hardware (computer) Healthy and to make the software as efficient as possible, By doing the Windows only (free) steps will prevent most performance related software support calls. Having said that by also including 3rd Party software will reduce the support calls even more. This strategy focuses on maintaining the Health of the PC which I believe is overlooked and underestimated by development teams.

Assuming the software will be deployed on a windows based platform there are several tools which can be used in order to provide a minimum maintenance strategy with little impact on the supplier these tools are, bearing in mind alot of support calls may be resolved by basic maintenance setting up a machine will prevent such support calls :-

  1. Task Scheduler enables maintenance to be done automatically meaning each machine can be configured based on the Business requirements.
  2. Disk Defragmenter should be scheduled Daily, note the more frequent it is scheduled the less there is to do, Optimizing the HDD regularly.
  3. Disk Cleanup Manager should be scheduled daily, note the more frequent it is scheduled the less there is to do, Meaning unwanted files will be removed regularly.
  4. Restart by scheduling the "shutdown -r" command at an appropriate time ensures the machine will be regularly restarted, Releasing memory on a regular basis.
  5. Security & Maintenance Section should be enabled within Windows.
  6. Windows Defender Should be enabled and configured within Windows, Basic antivirus (Better than nothing).
  7. Automatic Windows Updates should be configured to run automatically, keeping the operating system up to date.

There are 3rd party software titles which do more than the Windows Maintenance having said that they are usually chargeble, not limited to but some titles are :-

  1. Avira AV More advanced AV software takes precedence over Windows Defender.
  2. CCleaner More in Depth software to keep the PC healthy.
  3. Ease Us Suite Several Utilities again to keep the PC healthy.
It is Advised to review all the 3rd Party tools establishing the best value to be deployed with the sofware, as 3rd Party Software Providers will usually require a charge or donation.
